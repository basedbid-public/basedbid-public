# Why basedbid Can Win

basedbid is not a first-time launchpad experiment.

It is the next chapter from the same core team behind GemPad, one of the leading decentralized IDO launchpads. We have already entered an overcrowded market, been dismissed as "just another launchpad," competed against established incumbents, survived extreme pressure, and proved the market wrong.

Now we are applying that same execution playbook to a larger market: programmable token launch infrastructure.

## We Have Done This Before

The core team came together during the 2021 cycle.

At the time, the IDO launchpad market was already crowded. More than 100 launchpads were trying to launch, while PinkSale, UniCrypt, and DXsale had already established themselves as early market leaders.

They had brand recognition, liquidity, first-mover advantage, and the trust of projects and investors.

Everyone told us the same thing:

> "You are just another launchpad."

But we saw a market with massive demand and broken incentives.

The dominant platforms were taking advantage of market share, extracting heavily, allowing weak or malicious projects to pass through, and doing too little to improve the space. Projects needed better support, users needed better protection, and builders needed infrastructure that actually helped them succeed.

We believed we could do better.

So in early 2022, we dropped our original career plans and committed fully to building GemPad.

For six months, we built, shipped, supported projects, and pushed the platform forward without knowing whether it would become a real business or a failed attempt. The agreement was simple: either we became profitable, or we had wasted our time.

In May 2022, GemPad hit its revenue targets and we were finally able to start paying the team wages for their work.

That moment proved we could build launch infrastructure, compete in a crowded market, and turn it into a working business.

## GemPad Proved the Playbook

GemPad became one of the leading decentralized IDO launchpads.

From 2022 onward, we competed directly against established platforms such as PinkSale, UniCrypt, and DXsale. Those platforms started with the advantage. We started with conviction, execution, and a belief that launch infrastructure could be more responsible, more builder-focused, and more useful to the market.

Over the following years, GemPad supported thousands of clients, millions raised, and hundreds of millions in TVL.

The market also changed.

DXsale faded heavily from the category. UniCrypt became far more associated with liquidity locks and trust infrastructure than active launchpad dominance. PinkSale still exists, but public activity is far below the peak launchpad era.

The lesson was simple:

Being early is not enough. Having market share is not enough. Infrastructure wins when it keeps improving, keeps supporting builders, and keeps solving real problems.

## Battle-Tested Infrastructure

GemPad did not scale in a quiet market.

When we first disrupted the 8-9 figure IDO launchpad industry, we became a target. For months, GemPad faced sustained DDoS attacks during the same period independent cybersecurity publications were reporting some of the largest DDoS attacks in history.

The Hacker News covered Cloudflare’s report on record-breaking DDoS activity in June 2022, including a 26 million request-per-second HTTPS DDoS attack and a previous 15.3 million request-per-second HTTPS DDoS attack aimed at a crypto launchpad customer.

Reference: https://thehackernews.com/2022/06/cloudflare-saw-record-breaking-ddos.html

That period forced us to harden fast.

We upgraded server protections, rebuilt defensive layers, improved infrastructure resilience, and adapted our systems until the platform became increasingly difficult to take down.

Eventually, the attackers gave up.

That experience shaped how we think about infrastructure. Launch platforms are not normal web apps. They operate in adversarial environments where real money moves, competitors react, attackers probe for weakness, and communities expect uptime.

basedbid is built with that same mindset from day one.

## Why basedbid Exists

GemPad solved a major problem for the IDO era.

But the market has changed.

Token launches are no longer only about one-time fundraising events. Builders now need infrastructure that helps projects sustain themselves after launch.

The next problem is incentives.

Most token launch platforms still extract the economics away from the builders, creators, KOLs, and communities creating the demand. Projects launch, volume happens, platforms capture the fees, and everyone else is left fighting over token supply.

That model is broken.

basedbid is our answer to that problem.

Instead of only helping projects launch, basedbid helps them create sustainable post-launch economics.

## What basedbid Builds

basedbid is multi-chain token launch infrastructure for builders, creators, KOLs, communities, and launch partners.

It supports Liquidity Bonding Pools, instant token launches, programmable Fee Builder routing, native and stable-asset revenue distribution, Boards, custom market caps, DEX selection, post-launch monetization, governance transition, and creator-first revenue routing.

The core idea is simple:

> Most launch platforms help a token go live.  
> basedbid helps a token economy sustain itself after launch.

## The basedbid Difference

basedbid is designed around programmable launch economics.

Projects can define who earns, how fees are distributed, and how revenue is routed. Fees can support creator revenue, buybacks, liquidity, marketing, user rewards, KOL incentives, team wallets, DAO treasuries, and custom routing.

Fees can also be routed in native or stable assets, reducing the need for teams, KOLs, or contributors to sell project tokens to get paid.

This matters because sell pressure destroys trust.

basedbid gives projects a way to fund growth, marketing, rewards, and operations without relying on forced token dumping.

## Boards: The Distribution Flywheel

Boards turn distribution into infrastructure.

A Board is a programmable launch environment for KOLs, agencies, communities, incubators, and ecosystem partners.

Instead of promoting launches for one-time payments, a Board owner can create their own launch environment and earn from the activity they help generate.

KOLs can become launch partners. Communities can become launch networks. Agencies can create recurring revenue from the projects they support. Ecosystems can create curated launch environments for their builders.

Boards are how basedbid scales beyond one platform.
